Featured Study

Prayer and Faith

What Scripture Actually Teaches About How We Approach God

Most Christians inherit an idea of prayer rather than building one from Scripture. The common assumption is that prayer is a request system — you bring God a need or a desire, you ask sincerely, and if your faith is strong enough or your motives are pure enough, He grants it. When the answer doesn't come, people often reinterpret the outcome to fit what they prayed for, rather than asking whether they understood prayer correctly in the first place.

Featured Study

What Does Prayer Look Like?

What Scripture Models — From Jesus, Paul, and the Early Church

The question is not whether to pray. It is what prayer actually is. Scripture never describes prayer as a system for securing outcomes — ask sincerely enough, and the answer arrives. It describes something far more demanding: honest communion with the Father, total surrender to His will, and intercession that asks for the spiritual good of others rather than control over their circumstances.

Featured Study

Should We Seek Signs From God?

A Study on Coincidence, Confirmation, and Faith

Most of us have heard someone describe an experience like this: a particular bird shows up at just the right moment, a number keeps repeating, a dream feels too vivid to ignore, or something happens right after a prayer that seems to answer it exactly. The person telling the story usually isn't being dishonest or foolish — they're describing something that felt meaningful, often during a hard or emotional time, and they're reaching for language to make sense of it. "God sign," "god-wink," "coincidence that wasn't really a coincidence" — these are common ways people describe it.

Featured Study

The True Church According to Jesus

This is where the trail leads.

Strip away the buildings. Silence the stages. Dismantle the systems we've built in His name. What remains? Only what He actually called His Church. Not a place, but a people. Not a performance, but a priesthood. Not a building, but a body — formed by the Spirit, built on the foundation of Christ alone. We are not invited to redesign His Church. We are called to return to it. What follows is not a theory or an ideal — it is the shape of the Church as Jesus declared it. The kind He died to create. The kind that walks in power, because it walks in obedience. This is the Church the gates of hell cannot overcome. This is the Church that still burns with the fire of Acts. This is the Church that knows no altar but the cross. This is the true Church according to Jesus.
