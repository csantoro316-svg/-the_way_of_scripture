Passage

1 Timothy 6:16

LSB

"who alone is immortal and who lives in unapproachable light, whom no one has seen or can see. To him be honor and might forever. Amen."

Context

1 Timothy 6:16 should be read within the flow of its chapter and the larger argument of 1 Timothy.

Common Interpretations

The Father is never seen. He dwells in unapproachable light. No one has seen Him or can see Him. And yet the Old Testament is full of people seeing God — encountering Him visibly, physically, face to face. The resolution is precise: every visible appearance of God in the Old Testament is the second person of the Trinity — the eternal Son, appearing in human-like form before the Incarnation. He is the one who makes the invisible God visible and knowable. This is exactly what John 1:18 says: no one has seen God, but the Son has made Him known. He has been making Him known since the beginning. Every time you read "the Angel of the LORD" in your Old Testament, you are reading about the pre-incarnate Christ — present, active, and making the Father known long before Bethlehem.
