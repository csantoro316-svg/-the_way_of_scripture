Passage

Matthew 9:20-22

LSB

"And behold, a woman who had been suffering from a hemorrhage for twelve years, came up behind Him and touched the fringe of His cloak; for she was saying to herself, 'If I only touch His garment, I will get well.' But Jesus turning and seeing her said, 'Daughter, take courage; your faith has healed you.' And at once the woman was healed."

Context

In Matthew 9, Jesus' healing ministry reveals His authority to forgive, restore, and call sinners. Acts of healing in the chapter are repeatedly tied to faith and to the arrival of the kingdom in His person.

Common Interpretations

These two accounts are not identical, and the difference is worth naming rather than smoothing over. The woman never spoke a word to Jesus. She reasoned within herself, reached out, and touched His garment — pure faith, with no request voiced at all. The blind man's case is different: Jesus directly asked him, "What do you want Me to do for you?" and he answered specifically, "I want to regain my sight." Here a want was actually spoken aloud, in response to Christ's own question. But notice what Jesus does not say in either case. He does not say "because you asked correctly" or "because you asked at all." In both accounts — the one where nothing was asked, and the one where Jesus Himself drew out the request — His verdict is the same: "your faith has made you well." The presence or absence of a spoken request changes nothing about what Jesus credits. Faith is what He names both times. This is consistent with Hebrews 11:6 — what pleases God is seeking Him, not the words used in seeking Him.
