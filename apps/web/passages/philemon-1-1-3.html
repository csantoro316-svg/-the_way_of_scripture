Passage

Philemon 1:1-3

Greetings from Paul and Timothy

Berean Standard Bible

Paul, a prisoner of Christ Jesus, and Timothy our brother, To Philemon our beloved fellow worker, to Apphia our sister, to Archippus our fellow soldier, and to the church that meets at your house: Grace and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

Context

In Philemon 1, Paul writes a short but carefully crafted personal letter about reconciliation, fellowship, authority, and Christian love expressed in concrete action. Because the whole book is a single chapter, each section should be read as part of one sustained appeal.

Common Interpretations

Paul opens with warmth, but the greeting is already doing important work. He identifies himself not with apostolic force, but as "a prisoner of Christ Jesus" — framing the whole appeal in weakness, service, and cost rather than status. He also addresses not only Philemon, but Apphia, Archippus, and "the church that meets at your house." That matters. The issue is personal, but it is not purely private. Christian reconciliation belongs to the life of the ekklēsia.
