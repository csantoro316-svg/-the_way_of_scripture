Hebrews 7:25

Full passage text for this reference is not yet quoted in the current local source material.

Context

Hebrews 7:25 should be read within the flow of its chapter and the larger argument of Hebrews.

Common Interpretations

One priest. Permanent. No successor needed, because he never dies. And the practical consequence for every believer: Direct access. No human mediator standing between the believer and God. This is not a minor footnote — it's the reason a modern clergy class functioning as spiritual mediators between "regular" Christians and God is a step backward into a category Christ's priesthood already closed. The old covenant needed a priestly class because access was restricted. The new covenant abolished that restriction at the cross. Re-installing a mediating class — even informally, even with good intentions — argues against the very thing Hebrews spends ten chapters proving.
