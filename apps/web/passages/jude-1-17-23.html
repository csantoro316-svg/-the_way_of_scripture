Passage

Jude 1:17-23

A Call to Persevere

Berean Standard Bible

But you, beloved, remember what was foretold by the apostles of our Lord Jesus Christ when they said to you, “In the last times there will be scoffers who will follow after their own ungodly desires.” These are the ones who cause divisions, who are worldly and devoid of the Spirit. But you, beloved, by building yourselves up in your most holy faith and praying in the Holy Spirit, keep yourselves in the love of God as you await the mercy of our Lord Jesus Christ to bring you eternal life. And indeed, have mercy on those who doubt; save others by snatching them from the fire; and to still others show mercy tempered with fear, hating even the clothing stained by the flesh.

Context

After exposing the ungodly, Jude turns back to the faithful with a direct pastoral response. The section is practical and communal: remember apostolic teaching, build one another up, remain in God's love, and act with discernment toward others.

Common Interpretations

This section is commonly read as Jude's answer to corruption: not withdrawal into fear, but perseverance in truth, prayer, love, and mercy. The church is called both to guard itself and to care for those endangered by error. Verses 22-23 are often understood as requiring discernment in pastoral response. Different people are in different states of danger, and mercy must be applied wisely rather than mechanically.
