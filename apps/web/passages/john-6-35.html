Passage

John 6:35

LSB

'I am the bread of life; he who comes to Me will not hunger, and he who believes in Me will never thirst.'

Context

In John 6, Jesus moves from the feeding of the multitude into the Bread of Life discourse, where material bread becomes the doorway into deeper revelation about Himself. The chapter is intentionally interpretive: it takes a physical sign and presses the reader toward Christ as the true and lasting source of life.

Common Interpretations

Jesus is doing something very deliberate here. He is taking the manna tradition — the very background behind the daily bread petition in the Lord's Prayer — and explicitly placing Himself as its fulfillment. The manna kept Israel's bodies alive for a season in the wilderness, but everyone who ate it still eventually died. Jesus contrasts Himself directly with that manna: He is the bread that, once eaten, results in eternal life. The manna was never meant to be the end of the story. It was a sign pointing forward to Him all along. And lastly, the bread we are taught to ask for daily is not bread at all, but Christ Himself, the only sustenance that was ever truly necessary: "I am the bread of life; he who comes to Me will not hunger, and he who believes in Me will never thirst" (John 6:35). "Trust in Yahweh with all your heart and do not lean on your own understanding. In all your ways acknowledge Him, and He will make your paths straight." (Proverbs 3:5-6) That is what prayer is for.
