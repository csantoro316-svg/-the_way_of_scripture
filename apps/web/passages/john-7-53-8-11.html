John 7:53-8:11

Full passage text for this reference is not yet quoted in the current local source material.

Context

John 7:53-8:11 should be read within the flow of its chapter and the larger argument of John.

Common Interpretations

What remains is a genuinely small list of notable places where manuscripts differ in a way that affects the reading — the Comma Johanneum in 1 John 5:7-8 (an explicit Trinitarian formula found only in a handful of late manuscripts), the ending of Mark's Gospel after 16:8, and the story of the woman caught in adultery in John 7:53-8:11. These are worth knowing about, and good study Bibles typically flag them with a footnote. But even here, the core teaching involved — the Trinity, the resurrection, grace toward sinners — is affirmed clearly and repeatedly elsewhere in passages no one disputes. No textual variant anywhere in the manuscript tradition puts a single core doctrine of the faith at risk. That claim isn't ours alone — it's close to a consensus statement among textual critics across the theological spectrum, whatever position they hold on which text family is best. (For a genuinely exhaustive, manuscript-by-manuscript treatment of this subject, Berean Patriot's "Majority Text vs. Critical Text vs. Textus Receptus – Textual Criticism 101" is the most complete single resource we've found. It doesn't land firmly on any one camp, but it does the work of walking through the evidence in full. We link it here for anyone who wants to go deeper than this document is trying to.)
