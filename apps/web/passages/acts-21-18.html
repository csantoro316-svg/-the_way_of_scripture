Acts 21:18

Full passage text for this reference is not yet quoted in the current local source material.

Context

Acts 21:18 should be read within the flow of its chapter and the larger argument of Acts.

Common Interpretations

James, the Lord's brother, clearly carries real weight in Jerusalem. Paul names him among the "pillars" of the church (Galatians 2:9). At the Jerusalem council, James speaks last and delivers the summary judgment: "Therefore my judgment is that we should not trouble those of the Gentiles who turn to God" (Acts 15:19). When Paul later returns to Jerusalem, he goes to see James, and "all the elders were present" (Acts 21:18). So did Jerusalem have a head bishop, decades before the office supposedly developed? Look closer at how the text actually presents it. The Jerusalem council's decision is never James acting alone — it comes from "the apostles and the elders, with the whole church" (Acts 15:22), and the resulting letter is sent in the name of "the brothers, both the apostles and the elders" (Acts 15:23). James speaks with recognized authority, but the decision and the letter are corporate. And when Paul visits in Acts 21, James is never alone — the elders are present with him, functioning as a body, every time Scripture puts James in a leadership scene.
