Passage

Psalm 46:10

Full passage text for this reference is not yet quoted in the current local source material.

Context

Psalm 46 proclaims God's presence and stability when nations rage and the earth shakes. Its stillness language belongs to a psalm about God's sovereign rule and refuge, not to a merely inward or sentimental quietism.

Explanation

This topic connects directly to the broader question of how prayer and faith are described in Scripture — particularly the difference between faith that rests in what God has already done and said, versus prayer that seeks ongoing outward confirmation. See the companion documents Prayer and Faith and What Does Prayer Look Like? for more on that distinction, especially the discussion of the woman with the issue of blood and the pattern of faith without a requested sign. Also connected: the companion document What Does Prayer Look Like? addresses the question of sitting quietly and waiting for a feeling as God's answer — and why Psalm 46:10, often cited in support of that practice, actually says something very different in the Hebrew.

This study didn't start as an abstract topic. It started with a conversation. Someone close to me shared something good — an eagle, flying low, maybe chasing something in the yard. A genuinely nice moment. But the conversation didn't stop there. It moved into stories about dreams — reports of people having visions of Jesus, especially in certain countries — and something about that didn't sit right with me. It felt like it was reaching for more than Scripture actually supports. That feeling is what sent me into this study in the first place.

So I started digging into what the Bible actually says about signs, dreams, and how God communicates. And partway through that study, while I was working through the very passages about sign-seeking, I stopped. Because I remembered something I'd said in that same conversation, before any of this started — something about the eagle. I'd made a comment that maybe this was God's way of showing us a heads-up about something coming. I said I was being facetious. But I'd said it.
