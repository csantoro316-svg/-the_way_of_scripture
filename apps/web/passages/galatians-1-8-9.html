Passage

Galatians 1:8-9

LSB

"But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach a gospel contrary to the one we preached to you, let him be under a curse! As we have already said, so now I say again: If anyone is preaching to you a gospel contrary to the one you received, let him be under a curse!"

Context

Galatians 1:8-9 should be read within the flow of its chapter and the larger argument of Galatians.

Common Interpretations

Twice. The same warning, repeated in consecutive verses. Paul does not say "be careful about alternate gospels." He says let them be accursed. The strength of that language tells you how high the stakes are. The gospel is not a starting point that can be refined or reframed or supplemented with better ideas. It is a fixed proclamation. Change it, and you have not improved it. You have lost it. The core is the core. Everything else is built on top of it — or it is built on sand.
