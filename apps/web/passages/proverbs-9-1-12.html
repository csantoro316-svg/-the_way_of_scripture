Passage

Proverbs 9:1-12

The Way of Wisdom

Berean Standard Bible

1 Wisdom has built her house;

she has carved out her seven pillars.

2 She has prepared her meat and mixed her wine;

she has also set her table.

3 She has sent out her maidservants;

she calls out from the heights of the city.

4 “Whoever is simple, let him turn in here!”

she says to him who lacks judgment.

5 “Come, eat my bread

and drink the wine I have mixed.

6 Leave your folly behind, and you will live;

walk in the way of understanding.”

7 He who corrects a mocker brings shame on himself;

he who rebukes a wicked man taints himself.

8 Do not rebuke a mocker, or he will hate you;

rebuke a wise man, and he will love you.

9 Instruct a wise man, and he will be wiser still;

teach a righteous man, and he will increase his learning.

10 The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom,

and knowledge of the Holy One is understanding.

11 For through wisdom your days will be multiplied,

and years will be added to your life.

12 If you are wise, you are wise to your own advantage;

but if you scoff, you alone will bear the consequences.

Context

In Proverbs 9, this section should be read within the flow of the chapter and the larger movement of Proverbs.

Common Interpretations

Proverbs 9:10 places wisdom on the foundation of the fear of the LORD. In Scripture, that fear is not mere dread but reverent recognition of who God is. That is why true worship begins with holy awe before Him. Knowledge of the Holy One is not detached information; it is understanding shaped by right posture before God.

Current Position

This site's current position is that Proverbs 9:10 helps define the inner posture of worship more clearly than many modern worship frameworks do. Worship begins with the fear of the LORD because wisdom begins there. That means worship is not first a musical environment or an emotional state. It is a rightly ordered response to the holiness of God that then shapes everything else.
