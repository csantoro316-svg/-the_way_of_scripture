Context

Acts 6:1-6 should be read within the flow of its chapter and the larger argument of Acts.

Common Interpretations

Diakonos (deacon/servant) is different in kind, not rank — a recognized role of practical service (see 1 Timothy 3:8-13, Acts 6:1-6), never presented as a rung beneath elder in a chain of command, but as its own distinct function. None of these words appears in the New Testament used the way we use "Pastor" or "Bishop" today — as a formal title of address, a name someone is called by, a status held for life. Peter is never called "Bishop Peter." Paul never addresses a letter to "Bishop Timothy" or "Bishop Titus," even though both men were doing overseer-level work. They're addressed by name. Their function is described. The function never swallows the name. (For how the early church gathered and what happened when it did, see Worship as the Early Church Knew It for the fuller picture — the leadership question and the worship question are two sides of the same recovery.)
