Passage

2 John 1:4-6

Walking in the Truth

Berean Standard Bible

I was overjoyed to find some of your children walking in the truth, just as the Father has commanded us. And now I urge you, dear lady—not as a new commandment to you, but one we have had from the beginning—that we love one another. And this is love, that we walk according to His commandments. This is the very commandment you have heard from the beginning, that you must walk in love.

Context

John moves quickly from greeting to exhortation. The emphasis here is practical: truth is walked in, and love is defined by obedience rather than by feeling alone.

Common Interpretations

This section is commonly read as one of the clearest New Testament statements that love and obedience belong together. John does not oppose commandment-keeping to love; he treats obedience as the shape love takes. The repeated phrase "from the beginning" is usually understood as a reminder that John is not innovating. He is calling the church back to the apostolic pattern they already received.

Current Position

This site's current position is that 2 John 1:4-6 cuts directly against modern attempts to detach discipleship from obedience. John defines love by walking according to God's commandments, not by sincere feeling alone. A disciple is therefore not merely someone who admires Jesus, but someone who keeps walking in the truth He gave from the beginning.
