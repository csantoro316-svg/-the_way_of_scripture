Passage

2 John 1:7-11

Beware of Deceivers

Berean Standard Bible

For many deceivers have gone out into the world, refusing to confess the coming of Jesus Christ in the flesh. Any such person is the deceiver and the antichrist. Watch yourselves, so that you do not lose what we have worked for, but that you may be fully rewarded. Anyone who runs ahead without remaining in the teaching of Christ does not have God. Whoever remains in His teaching has both the Father and the Son. If anyone comes to you but does not bring this teaching, do not receive him into your home or even greet him. Whoever greets such a person shares in his evil deeds.

Context

This is the warning center of the letter. John is not addressing minor disagreement, but Christological deviation tied to active deception and communal danger.

Common Interpretations

A common interpretation of this passage is that John is drawing a boundary around core apostolic Christology. The issue is not personal preference or secondary disagreement, but denial of the true coming of Christ in the flesh. Verses 10-11 are often misunderstood as a ban on ordinary courtesy toward any non-Christian. More commonly, interpreters understand the warning as directed against granting ministry welcome, endorsement, or platform to a false teacher.

Current Position

This site's current position is that 2 John 1:7-11 shows the church must have real doctrinal boundaries. Love does not require platforming or endorsing those who deny core apostolic truth about Christ. The command is not a ban on ordinary human kindness. It is a refusal to participate in or legitimize false teaching in the life of the church.
