Passage

3 John 1:1-4

A Greeting from the Elder

Berean Standard Bible

The elder, To the beloved Gaius, whom I love in the truth: Beloved, I pray that in every way you may prosper and enjoy good health, as your soul also prospers. For I was overjoyed when the brothers came and testified about your devotion to the truth, in which you continue to walk. I have no greater joy than to hear that my children are walking in the truth.

Context

In 3 John 1, John writes to Gaius about faithful conduct, harmful leadership, and the importance of supporting those who labor for the truth. The letter is personal, but its concerns are unmistakably ecclesial.

Common Interpretations

A common interpretation of the opening is that John is not offering a prosperity formula, but expressing pastoral affection and blessing. The emphasis quickly settles on spiritual faithfulness, which is the real measure of joy in the passage. John's repeated language of truth is usually understood as more than sincerity. It points to a shared apostolic reality that governs both relationship and conduct.
