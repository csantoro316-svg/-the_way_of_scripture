Passage

Romans 10:9-10

LSB

"if you confess with your mouth, 'Jesus is Lord,' and believe in your heart that God raised Him from the dead, you will be saved. For with your heart you believe and are justified, and with your mouth you confess and are saved."

Context

In Romans 10, Paul is explaining righteousness by faith, the nearness of the word, and the necessity of confession and belief in Christ. The chapter belongs to his larger argument about the gospel, Israel, and salvation through faith rather than law.

Common Interpretations

This is the response: repent, believe, confess Jesus as Lord, and call on Him. Salvation is not inherited from family, achieved by moral effort, or secured by religious activity. It is received by turning to Jesus Christ in faith and trusting entirely in what He has done through His death and resurrection. If today is the day you are ready to do that, then call on Him. There is no magic wording that saves a person. The Lord is not listening for a formula. He is looking at the heart.
