Passage

Genesis 16:1-16

Hagar and Ishmael

Berean Standard Bible

1 Now Abram’s wife Sarai had borne him no children, but she had an Egyptian maidservant named Hagar. 2 So Sarai said to Abram, “Look now, the LORD has prevented me from bearing children. Please go to my maidservant;perhaps I can build a family by her.”

And Abram listened to the voice of Sarai. 3 So after he had lived in Canaan for ten years, his wife Sarai took her Egyptian maidservant Hagar and gave her to Abram to be his wife. 4 And he slept with Hagar, and she conceived. But when Hagar realized that she was pregnant, she began to despise her mistress.

5 Then Sarai said to Abram, “May the wrong done to me be upon you! I delivered my servant into your arms, and ever since she saw that she was pregnant, she has treated me with contempt. May the LORD judge between you and me.”

6 “Here,” said Abram, “your servant is in your hands. Do whatever you want with her.” Then Sarai treated Hagar so harshly that she fled from her.

7 Now the angel of the LORD found Hagar by a spring of water in the desert—the spring along the road to Shur. 8 “Hagar, servant of Sarai,” he said, “where have you come from, and where are you going?”

“I am running away from my mistress Sarai,” she replied.

9 So the angel of the LORD told her, “Return to your mistress and submit to her authority.” 10 Then the angel added, “I will greatly multiply your offspring so that they will be too numerous to count.”

11 The angel of the LORD proceeded:

“Behold, you have conceived and will bear a son.

And you shall name him Ishmael,

for the LORD has heard your cry of affliction.

12 He will be a wild donkey of a man,

and his hand will be against everyone,

and everyone’s hand against him;

he will live in hostility

toward all his brothers.”

13 So Hagar gave this name to the LORD who had spoken to her: “You are the God who sees me,” for she said, “Here I have seen the One who sees me!” 14 Therefore the well was called Beer-lahai-roi. It is located between Kadesh and Bered.

15 And Hagar bore Abram a son, and Abram gave the name Ishmael to the son she had borne. 16 Abram was eighty-six years old when Hagar bore Ishmael to him.

Context

In Genesis 16, Abram and Sarai respond to delay in God's promise by taking matters into their own hands through Hagar. The chapter exposes the pain and disorder that follow when people try to secure God's promise by human strategy.

Common Interpretations

A common reading of this section is that the delay of the promise becomes the testing ground of faith. Sarai and Abram do not reject God's promise outright; they attempt to achieve it by their own arrangement, and the rest of the chapter shows the disorder that follows. Interpreters often treat the line "Abram listened to the voice of Sarai" as part of the narrative warning. The problem is not ordinary spousal counsel, but a human plan substituted for patient trust in what God said He would do.

Current Position

This site's current position is that Genesis 16 shows what happens when people try to force God's promise into being by human means. Abram and Sarai do not deny the promise; they try to accomplish it on their own terms, and the chapter records the damage that follows. At the same time, the chapter also becomes the first appearance of the Angel of the LORD. That matters because the same chapter that exposes human impatience also contains a direct appearance of the One Hagar identifies as God Himself.
