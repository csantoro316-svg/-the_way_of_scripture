Passage

James 5:14-16

LSB

"Is anyone among you sick? Then he must call for the elders of the church and they are to pray over him, anointing him with oil in the name of the Lord; and the prayer offered in faith will restore the one who is sick, and the Lord will raise him up, and if he has committed sins, they will be forgiven him. Therefore, confess your sins to one another, and pray for one another so that you may be healed. The effective prayer of a righteous man can accomplish much."

Context

In James 5, James closes the letter by addressing suffering, patience, oaths, confession, and prayer in the life of the community. The chapter presents prayer in the setting of endurance, restoration, righteousness, and life together before God.

Common Interpretations

This passage is commonly read as an unconditional promise of physical healing through prayer — pray in faith over the sick, and God will restore them. That reading collapses under the weight of the Greek word James actually uses. The word translated "restore" is σῴζω (sōzō (SOH-zoh)) — the standard New Testament word for salvation and spiritual wholeness. It is the same word used throughout the Gospels and Paul's letters for being saved. James does not use the ordinary Greek word for physical recovery. He uses the word for spiritual restoration — and the passage itself confirms this is his meaning, because he immediately connects the prayer to the forgiveness of sins and the confession of sins to one another. You do not confess sins to break a fever. You confess sins to restore fellowship with God.
