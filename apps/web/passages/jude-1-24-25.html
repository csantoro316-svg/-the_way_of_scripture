Passage

Jude 1:24-25

Doxology

Berean Standard Bible

Now to Him who is able to keep you from stumbling and to present you unblemished in His glorious presence, with great joy—to the only God our Savior be glory, majesty, dominion, and authority through Jesus Christ our Lord before all time, and now, and for all eternity.

Context

Jude ends not in anxiety, but in worship. After a letter full of warning, the final emphasis falls on God's preserving power and eternal glory.

Common Interpretations

A common reading of this doxology is that it answers the weight of the whole letter: the church must contend and persevere, but its final security rests in the God who is able to keep His people and present them blameless. The ending is therefore both theological and pastoral. It lifts the reader's eyes from danger to the supremacy and faithfulness of God.

Current Position

This site's current position is that Jude ends exactly where biblical prayer and faith should end: not in confidence in the believer's own grip on God, but in confidence in God's ability to keep His people. That does not eliminate responsibility or endurance, but it does relocate the center of trust. The final note is worshipful dependence on the God who preserves, presents, and receives glory.
