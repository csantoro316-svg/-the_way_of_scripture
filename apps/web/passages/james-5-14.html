Passage

James 5:14

Full passage text for this reference is not yet quoted in the current local source material.

Context

In James 5, James closes the letter by addressing suffering, patience, oaths, confession, and prayer in the life of the community. The chapter presents prayer in the setting of endurance, restoration, righteousness, and life together before God.

Common Interpretations

The Greek word προσκυνέω (proskuneō (PROS-koo-neh-oh)), which means to bow down or prostrate oneself in homage, is rendered generically as "worship" — collapsing a specific, embodied act of submission into a vague modern concept of religious feeling. The word λατρεύω (latreuō (lat-REW-oh)), meaning priestly service or sacred vocation, is similarly flattened to "worship" — erasing the distinction between a lifestyle of holy service and an hour on Sunday morning. The word ἀσθενέω (asthenoō (as-theh-NEH-oh)), meaning "weak," is rendered "sick" in James 5:14 — a choice that generated an entire theology of miraculous physical healing from a passage that may have been addressing spiritual restoration.
