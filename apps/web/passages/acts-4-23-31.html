Passage

Acts 4:23-31

Context

In Acts 4, the apostles face threats after proclaiming Christ publicly, and the church responds together in prayer. The chapter is framed by opposition, bold witness, and the sovereign rule of God, so its prayer language is bound to mission rather than self-protection.

Common Interpretations

Peter and John have just been released from prison after being threatened by the religious authorities for preaching. The believers gather together, and they pray. What they ask for says everything about how the earliest church understood prayer under real pressure. Read the full passage in your Bible (Acts 4:23-31). Key verses are quoted below. They do not pray for the threats to stop. They do not pray for protection, or for their circumstances to improve. They begin by acknowledging exactly who God is — Creator of heaven and earth — and then ask for one thing: boldness to keep speaking, whatever comes. They surrender the outcome entirely and ask only for the courage to remain faithful inside it. This is the early church's answer to hardship — not "Lord, remove this," but "Lord, make us faithful in it." Paul asks for the same thing from prison years later: not release, not relief from his chains, but boldness to keep proclaiming the gospel in spite of them (Ephesians 6:19-20). The pattern holds from the first chapters of Acts to the last of Paul's letters — when believers are under real pressure, what they consistently ask for is not an exit from the pressure, but the courage to remain faithful inside it.
