Passage

Titus 3:9-11

Avoid Divisions

Berean Standard Bible

But avoid foolish controversies, genealogies, arguments, and quarrels about the law, because these things are pointless and worthless. Reject a divisive man after a first and second admonition, knowing that such a man is corrupt and sinful; he is self-condemned.

Context

After emphasizing salvation and fruitful living, Paul returns to the kinds of disputes that fracture communities without producing godliness. The warning is not against careful biblical reasoning, but against empty controversy.

Common Interpretations

A common interpretation here is that Paul distinguishes profitable truth from argumentative religion. Some disputes clarify the faith; the controversies named here consume energy while producing no real fruit. The command to reject a divisive person after repeated warning is often read as a guard for the health of the assembly, not a license for impulsive exclusion.
