Passage

Acts 8:38-39

LSB

"And he ordered the chariot to stop; and they both went down into the water, Philip as well as the eunuch, and he baptized him. When they came up out of the water, the Spirit of the Lord snatched Philip away."

Context

In Acts 8, the gospel is moving beyond Jerusalem, and Philip's ministry among the Samaritans and the Ethiopian shows the widening reach of Christ's kingdom. The chapter's baptism scenes belong to that outward expansion of the gospel and the immediate obedience of those who receive it.

Common Interpretations

Down into the water. Up out of the water. This is the consistent physical picture of baptism in the NT narrative. Much water was needed — consistent with immersion as the norm. Sprinkling requires no such provision.
