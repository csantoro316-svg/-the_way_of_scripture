Passage

2 John 1:12-13

Conclusion

Berean Standard Bible

I have many things to write to you, but I would prefer not to do so with paper and ink. Instead, I hope to come and speak with you face to face, so that our joy may be complete. The children of your elect sister send you greetings.

Context

John closes the letter by reminding the readers that written warning is not the whole of pastoral life. Truth is also carried in direct fellowship and embodied presence.

Common Interpretations

A common reading of the conclusion is that John values personal presence because some things are best settled in face-to-face fellowship rather than through distance alone. The letter is brief by design, not by lack of concern. The closing greeting also reinforces that local believers and local churches do not stand alone. The life of truth and love is shared across the wider body.
