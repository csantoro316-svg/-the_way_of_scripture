Passage

Jeremiah 29:11-13

LSB

"'For I know the plans that I have for you,' declares the Lord, 'plans for well-being and not for calamity, to give you a future and a hope. Then you will call upon Me and come and pray to Me, and I will listen to you. And you will seek Me and find Me when you search for Me with all your heart.'"

Context

In Jeremiah 29, the Lord speaks to exiles already in Babylon, commanding them to live faithfully there while awaiting His appointed restoration. The chapter is covenantal and historical, not a free-floating promise detached from exile, judgment, and God's long-range purpose for His people.

Explanation

This is arguably the most universally misapplied passage in the entire Bible. It appears on coffee mugs, graduation cards, motivational posters, and church bulletins as a personal promise of prosperity, blessing, and a bright future — addressed to whoever is reading it.

Almost none of that survives contact with the actual context.
