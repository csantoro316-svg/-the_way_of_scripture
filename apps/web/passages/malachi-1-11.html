Malachi 1:11

Full passage text for this reference is not yet quoted in the current local source material.

Context

Malachi 1:11 should be read within the flow of its chapter and the larger argument of Malachi.

Explanation

The most consequential theological shift of the Constantinian era was the reinterpretation of the Lord's Supper as a sacrifice. Early in this period, Malachi 1:11 — "in every place incense is going to be offered to My name, and a grain offering that is pure" — was applied to the Eucharist as a fulfillment of universal, ongoing sacrifice. Once the Lord's Supper was framed as a sacrifice, the logic of Ferguson's four-element framework played out inevitably:

  • A sacrifice needs an altar → communion tables were repositioned and architecturally elevated as altars
  • An altar needs a priest → a professional, ordained priesthood was established to preside over the sacrifice
  • A priest needs a temple → sacred buildings were constructed to house the altar and the priestly function

Scholar I. Howard Marshall noted that this sacrificial reinterpretation "reverses the direction of atonement." In the NT, God provides the sacrifice moving toward humanity — Christ dying for human sin. In the emerging Mass theology, humanity offers a sacrifice moving toward God — Christ being offered again to obtain divine favor. The direction is inverted. What the new covenant accomplished once for all is re-enacted as an ongoing human offering, rather than received as a completed divine gift.
