Context

Hebrews 5:6 should be read within the flow of its chapter and the larger argument of Hebrews.

Common Interpretations

The book of Hebrews spends its middle chapters establishing that Jesus is a priest "according to the order of Melchizedek" (Hebrews 5:6, quoting Psalm 110:4) rather than according to the order of Levi. Melchizedek appears once in Genesis, blessing Abraham — a king and priest with no recorded genealogy, no predecessor, no successor (Genesis 14:18-20; see also Hebrews 7:1-3). The writer of Hebrews uses that silence deliberately: a priesthood that isn't passed down, isn't inherited, isn't mediated through a class of men set apart by birth. Then comes the argument that matters most for this study: A change in priesthood. A change in law. The old covenant required a mediating class — men set apart, functioning between God and the people, offering sacrifices the people themselves could not offer.
