Passage

2 Corinthians 5:7

LSB

"for we walk by faith, not by sight."

Context

In 2 Corinthians 5, Paul speaks about life between the present age and the coming fullness of resurrection glory. The chapter is saturated with faith, hope, reconciliation, and the unseen realities of belonging to Christ, so its famous lines must be read in that eschatological and gospel-centered frame.

Common Interpretations

A note on context: this exchange is sometimes confused with the Beatitudes of the Sermon on the Mount, but it takes place in an entirely different setting — Jesus appearing to the disciples after the resurrection, specifically addressing Thomas's demand to physically see and touch the wounds before he would believe. The principle fits alongside 2 Corinthians 5:7: both describe a faith that does not depend on first receiving physical proof or confirmation. Observation: Scripture consistently describes the normal Christian life as one of trusting what God has already revealed in His Word — not one of collecting ongoing proofs to keep that trust going. A faith that needs a new confirmation every week to stay standing is not the kind of faith these passages describe.
