Passage

Titus 2:11-15

God's Grace Brings Salvation

Berean Standard Bible

For the grace of God has appeared, bringing salvation to everyone. It instructs us to renounce ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live sensible, upright, and godly lives in the present age, as we await the blessed hope and glorious appearance of our great God and Savior Jesus Christ. He gave Himself for us to redeem us from all lawlessness and to purify for Himself a people for His own possession, zealous for good deeds. Speak these things as you encourage and rebuke with all authority. Let no one despise you.

Context

Paul grounds the ethical demands of chapter 2 in the appearing of God's grace. Grace is not introduced here as permission to remain unchanged, but as the power and pattern for a transformed life.

Common Interpretations

A common interpretation of this passage is that grace both saves and trains. Paul does not set salvation by grace against holiness; he presents grace itself as teaching believers how to renounce ungodliness and live rightly. The statement about "our great God and Savior Jesus Christ" is also widely recognized as a strong affirmation of Christ's deity within the flow of Paul's pastoral instruction.
