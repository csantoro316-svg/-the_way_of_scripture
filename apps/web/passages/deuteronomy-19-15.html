Passage

Deuteronomy 19:15

Full passage text for this reference is not yet quoted in the current local source material.

Context

In Deuteronomy 19, Moses gives laws meant to preserve justice and truth within Israel's covenant life. The chapter's witness standard belongs to that broader concern that judgment must rest on established testimony rather than private accusation or impulse.

Explanation

The "two or three" language is a direct echo of Deuteronomy 19:15 — "A single witness shall not rise up against a man on account of any iniquity or any sin... on the evidence of two or three witnesses a matter shall be confirmed." Jesus is applying the Old Testament standard for legal testimony to the process of church discipline. He is not establishing a prayer formula.

"Where two or three have gathered together in My name, I am there in their midst" is similarly misread. It is not saying that small prayer groups have a special power unavailable to individuals. It is saying that when the church gathers in His name to exercise the authority He has given it — including the difficult work of discipline and restoration — He is present in that process, authorizing and confirming what is done faithfully.

What this means for prayer is simply that it doesn't mean anything for prayer — at least not what is commonly claimed. Two people agreeing on something that is not according to God's will does not become according to His will because two people agreed on it.
