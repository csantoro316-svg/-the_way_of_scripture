Passage

Titus 3:12-15

Final Remarks and Greetings

Berean Standard Bible

As soon as I send Artemas or Tychicus to you, make every effort to come to me at Nicopolis, because I have decided to winter there. Do your best to equip Zenas the lawyer and Apollos, so that they will have everything they need. And our people must also learn to devote themselves to good works in order to meet the pressing needs of others, so that they will not be unfruitful. All who are with me send you greetings. Greet those who love us in the faith. Grace be with all of you.

Context

Titus closes with travel plans, provision for co-laborers, and final greetings. Even the letter's practical ending reinforces Paul's pattern: doctrine, mission, fellowship, and tangible support belong together.

Common Interpretations

A common reading of this conclusion is that ordinary logistical details are part of faithful ministry rather than distractions from it. The gospel moves through real people, real needs, and real generosity. The call to devote themselves to good works is often seen as the letter's closing refrain, showing that Titus ends where it has been aiming all along: truth producing fruitful lives.
