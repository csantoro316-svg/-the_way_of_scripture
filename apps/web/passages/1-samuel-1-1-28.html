Context

In 1 Samuel 1, the narrative centers on Hannah's distress, her prayer before the Lord, and the birth of Samuel. The chapter is not merely about private desire being granted; it sets up God's larger work in Israel through the child who will become prophet, judge, and pivotal figure in the transition toward monarchy.

Explanation

Read the full passage (1 Samuel 1:1-28). Key verses below.

Three things the passage actually shows:

First — Hannah's prayer was not a personal request for personal fulfillment. She vowed to give the child back to God entirely — Samuel would be dedicated to the Lord's service for life. She was asking for a son to give to God. The request was already oriented toward God's purposes, not her own.
