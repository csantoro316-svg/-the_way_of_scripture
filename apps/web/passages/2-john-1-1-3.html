Passage

2 John 1:1-3

A Greeting from the Elder

Berean Standard Bible

The elder, To the chosen lady and her children, whom I love in the truth—and not I alone, but also all who know the truth—because of the truth that abides in us and will be with us forever: Grace, mercy, and peace from God the Father and from Jesus Christ, the Son of the Father, will be with us in truth and love.

Context

In 2 John 1, John writes briefly but pointedly about truth, love, obedience, and the danger of receiving false teaching. The letter is pastoral and relational, but it is also doctrinally sharp.

Common Interpretations

A common interpretation of the opening is that John joins truth and love from the very start so they cannot be separated later in the letter. Christian love is not treated as sentiment detached from doctrine, and truth is not treated as cold abstraction detached from love. The identity of the "chosen lady" is interpreted in more than one way. Some read it as a literal Christian woman and her household; others read it as a local church personified as a lady. Either way, the letter is clearly addressed to a real believing community under pastoral concern.

Current Position

This site's current position is that 2 John 1:1-3 establishes a pattern the church still needs: truth and love are not competing virtues. They belong together from the opening line. That matters for how the church is identified. A true church cannot preserve love by minimizing truth, nor preserve truth while treating love as optional.
