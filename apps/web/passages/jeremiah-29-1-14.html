Passage

Jeremiah 29:1-14

Full passage text for this reference is not yet quoted in the current local source material.

Context

In Jeremiah 29, the Lord speaks to exiles already in Babylon, commanding them to live faithfully there while awaiting His appointed restoration. The chapter is covenantal and historical, not a free-floating promise detached from exile, judgment, and God's long-range purpose for His people.

Explanation

Read the full passage (Jeremiah 29:1-14). Jeremiah is writing a specific letter to a specific people in a specific situation — the Israelites already carried into exile in Babylon. Verse 10 makes the timeline explicit: "When seventy years have been completed for Babylon, I will visit you and fulfill My good word to you." The promise of verses 11-13 is God's assurance to a specific exiled nation that after seventy years of captivity He will restore them. It is a promise about national restoration after a defined period of judgment, not a personal promise to any individual reader.

The word "then" in verse 12 is critical — "Then you will call upon Me." It points to a specific future moment after the seventy years are complete, not a general open-ended promise that God listens whenever anyone prays. And verse 13: "You will seek Me and find Me when you search for Me with all your heart" — this is not a promise that seeking God produces desired outcomes. It is a promise that genuine, wholehearted seeking of God — not seeking outcomes from God — will find Him. That is entirely consistent with everything the prayer documents establish.
